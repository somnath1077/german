German Cases
*********************************************************************************

Cases indicate the role that nouns and pronouns play in a sentence. In German, 
the following four types of words: (1) nouns, (2) pronouns, (3) articles, and 
(4) adjectives are spelt differently depending on the case that they represent 
in a sentence.

German has four cases: (1) Nominative, (2) Accusative, (3) Dative, (4) Genetive.

NOMINATIVE
***********

The nominative case describes the subject of a sentence. It answers the question:
"who?" or "wer?"

ACCUSATIVE
**********

The accusative case describes the direct object of a sentence. It answers the 
question: "whom?" or "wen?". Some prepositions always take the accusative case.
These are:

* durch 
* bis
* für 
* ohne
* entlang (along)
* gegen (against)
* um (around)

Masculine | den        | ihn
Feminine  | die        | sie
Neutral   | das        | es
Plural    | die        | sie/Sie

DATIVE
******

The dative case describes the indirect object of the sentence and answers the 
question "wem?" (whom) or "was?" (what).

Masculine | dem        | ihm
Feminine  | der        | ihr
Neutral   | dem        | ihm
Plural    | den ... n  | ihnen/Ihnen

Certain verbs must be used only with the Dativ case. Some of these are:

* antworten
* danken
* folgen
* fehlen
* gehören
* gefallen 
* gratulieren
* glauben
* helfen
* schmecken
* vertrauen
* zuhören

Several prepositions take the dative case:

* aus (out)
* auβer (besides) 
* bei (next to) 
* mit (with) 
* nach (after)
* seit (since) 
* von (from) 
* zu (to) 
* gegenüber (opposite) 

GENETIVE
********


The genetive is used to show possession, ownership and close relationship. It 
answers the question "wessen?" (whose).

Masculine | des        
Feminine  | der        
Neutral   | des        
Plural    | der      

Certain prepositions always take the genetive case:

* anstatt (instead of)
* außerhalb (outside of)
* innerhalb (inside of)
* trotz (despite)
* während (during)
* wegen (because of)
